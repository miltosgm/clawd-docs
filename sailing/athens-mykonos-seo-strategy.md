## Executive Summary

Athens Sailing and Mykonos Sailing operate as a unified two-base luxury charter business in Greece. Their core product is premium private and semi-private yacht/catamaran experiences along the Athens Riviera and Cyclades. Both sites are currently under-indexed given the volume of high-intent travel searches passing through these markets.

The opportunity is significant: OTA platforms (GetYourGuide, TripAdvisor, Viator) dominate the top organic results, while direct-booking competitors like sailingathens.com and setsailmykonos.com are relatively thin on content. With the right SEO and content investment, both domains can capture high-intent traffic and reduce dependency on OTA commission fees (typically 20–30%).

**Estimated organic revenue opportunity (12 months):** 30–50 additional direct bookings/month at avg. €600–1,200 per booking = €18,000–60,000/month in incremental direct revenue.

## 2. Google Search Console Priority Actions

*Assume GSC access is being set up / recently established. These are ordered by impact.*

### 2.1 Immediate Technical Fixes (Week 1)

**1. Verify both properties in GSC**
- athenssailing.gr (primary)
- mykonossailing.gr (primary)
- Also verify any www. variants and ensure canonical tags point to preferred URL

**2. Submit XML Sitemaps**
- Generate and submit `/sitemap.xml` for both domains
- Ensure all key service pages, fleet pages, and blog posts are included
- Exclude thank-you, admin, and duplicate pages

**3. Fix Core Web Vitals failures**
- Use GSC "Page Experience" report to identify failing URLs
- Target LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) < 2.5s — yacht/boat photo-heavy sites commonly fail here
- Compress hero images (WebP format, lazy-load below fold)
- Defer non-critical JS (booking widgets, chat)

**4. Fix Crawl Errors**
- In GSC > Coverage report: fix all 4xx errors immediately (broken links, old pages)
- Set up 301 redirects for any renamed service pages
- Resolve any "Submitted but Blocked" or "Excluded" pages that should be indexed

**5. Mobile Usability**
- GSC Mobile Usability report — fix any "clickable elements too close together" (common on booking buttons)
- Ensure tap targets are ≥ 48x48px
- Text too small to read: minimum 16px body text

### 2.2 Search Performance Optimization (Month 1–2)

**6. Identify Quick-Win Pages**
In GSC > Search Results: filter by position 8–20 for queries with >50 impressions/month. These are pages one optimization push away from page 1.
- Improve title tags and H1 for these pages
- Add internal links from high-authority pages
- Add FAQ schema to boost CTR

**7. Fix Title Tags & Meta Descriptions**
GSC will show pages with low CTR despite good rankings. Rewrite:
- Title tags: include primary keyword + location + USP (e.g., "Private Catamaran Athens Riviera | Athens Sailing")
- Meta descriptions: Add CTA + social proof (e.g., "★ 5.0 Reviews · Private & Semi-Private · Food Included · Book Direct & Save")

**8. Track Branded vs. Non-Branded Split**
- Filter GSC impressions by brand name (Athens Sailing, Mykonos Sailing) vs. non-brand
- Non-brand percentage should grow month-over-month as organic strategy executes
- Target: 40%+ non-brand traffic within 6 months

**9. International Impressions**
- Check GSC "Countries" tab — UK, US, Germany, Australia likely top sources
- Ensure hreflang is NOT needed (single English site) or add Greek/German versions if data warrants

**10. Index Coverage Monitoring**
- Weekly check: ensure new blog posts get indexed within 7 days
- Use URL Inspection Tool to request indexing for new content
- Flag any pages showing "Discovered - not indexed" for >14 days

## 5. AEO/GEO: Appearing in AI Search Results

*AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude) is now a primary discovery channel for travel planning. These platforms pull from structured, authoritative, conversational content.*

### 5.1 Why This Matters Right Now

When someone asks ChatGPT "What's the best way to see Delos from Mykonos?" or asks Google "best private sailing tours Athens" — the AI overview answers pull from:
1. Well-structured FAQ content
2. High-authority domain signals
3. Review-rich listings (TripAdvisor, Google)
4. Content that directly answers the question conversationally

Neither athenssailing.gr nor mykonossailing.gr currently appear in AI overviews for their top queries. This is the single biggest unlocked growth channel.

### 5.2 Concrete AEO Actions

**A. Answer the exact questions AI tools get asked**

Create content that answers:
- "What is the best sailing tour in Athens?"
- "How do I book a private boat in Mykonos?"
- "Is it worth getting a private charter vs a group tour in Greece?"
- "What's included in an Athens sailing day trip?"
- "How much does a private sailing charter in Mykonos cost?"
- "Can I sail from Athens to Mykonos?"

Each should be answered in a clear, first-person authoritative way within your existing service pages and blog posts. Put the answer in the first paragraph, then elaborate.

**B. Structured Q&A Format (FAQ Schema)**

Every service page must include 5–8 FAQ items using FAQPage schema. Google AI Overviews pull heavily from these. Example for /mykonos-catamaran-tour/:

```
Q: What is included in a Mykonos catamaran tour?
A: Our Mykonos catamaran tours include a private skipper, 
   fuel, snorkeling equipment, towels, Greek mezze lunch, 
   and unlimited soft drinks. Alcoholic beverages available 
   for an additional fee. No hidden costs.
```

**C. Build E-E-A-T Signals (Critical for AI citation)**

AI tools cite sources they consider credible. To get cited:
- Add a team/about page: "X years operating in the Aegean, licensed by Greek Maritime Authority, [certification]"
- Publish content signed by named crew/experts (not "admin")
- Get featured/mentioned on: travel magazines (Forbes Travel, CN Traveller, Condé Nast), yachting publications, local Greek news
- Respond to HARO (Help a Reporter Out) queries about Greece travel
- Ensure schema markup is flawless (validates on schema.org validator)

**D. Perplexity & ChatGPT Optimization**

These AI tools increasingly return specific brand recommendations when:
- Your brand is mentioned in multiple credible third-party sources
- You have a Wikipedia or notable mention on high-authority domains
- Your TripAdvisor and Google review profiles are strong and recent

Target: get mentioned in 5+ travel blog articles (see link building section) that specifically name Athens Sailing / Mykonos Sailing. These citations feed AI training data and real-time retrieval.

**E. Google AI Overviews**

Target queries where AI Overviews currently appear:
- "sailing tours Athens Greece"
- "Mykonos boat tours"
- "private yacht charter Greece day trip"

To appear in overviews: create content that comprehensively answers these queries with lists, numbered steps, and direct answers. AI overviews prefer content that is already ranking page 1 AND has strong FAQ schema.

**F. "People Also Ask" Ownership**

Research PAA boxes for your top 20 queries. Write dedicated sections or FAQ items targeting each PAA question. Owning 5–10 PAA boxes per target keyword significantly increases AI visibility.
