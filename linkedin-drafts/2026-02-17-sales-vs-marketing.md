**The Root Problem:**

No SLA (Service Level Agreement) between teams.

No definition of what "quality" means.

No metrics that matter.

Result: Both teams optimizing for different things.

**Example:**

Marketing says: "We got 500 leads this month"

Sales says: "Most of them are worthless"

Both are right.

Marketing optimized for volume. Sales needs qualified leads.

Different targets = different behaviors = conflict.

**The SLA Framework:**

**1. Define lead quality together**

Not marketing alone. Not sales alone. Together.

"A qualified lead is:"
- Specific role (VP Growth, not "hiring manager")
- Right company size ($5M-$50M ARR, not startup or enterprise)
- Active buying signal (opened 3+ emails, visited pricing page)
- Timeline to decision (buying in next 60 days, not someday)
- Budget authority (can approve, not evaluating)

Write this down. Agree on it.

**2. Create handoff metrics**

Marketing delivers:
- [X leads per month]
- [Y% meet quality criteria]
- [Z% convert to sales calls]

Sales commits to:
- Call back within [X hours]
- Qualify or disqualify within [X days]
- Provide feedback on lead quality

Both teams now have accountability.

**3. Measure what matters**

Not just revenue (too slow to adjust).

- Meeting â†’ Opportunity: [% that convert to opp]

Where's the biggest drop-off?

That's where to optimize.

**Real example:**

Company A:

- Marketing: 500 leads/month
- Sales: 50% conversion to meeting
- Meeting: 30% conversion to opportunity
- Opportunity: 20% close rate
- Result: 1,500 revenue per month

Company B (same marketing):

- Marketing: 500 leads/month
- Sales: 80% conversion to meeting (better qualification)
- Meeting: 40% conversion to opportunity (better pitch)
- Opportunity: 35% close rate (better negotiation)
- Result: 5,600 revenue per month

Same leads. 3.7x better outcome.

**The uncomfortable truth:**

Most revenue problems aren't "not enough leads."

They're "not enough follow-up" or "not enough conversion."

But it's easier to blame marketing for "low quality" than to fix your sales process.
