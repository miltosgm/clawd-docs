**Here's what changed:**

Google Search: You compete for position #1 in a list.

AI Search: You compete to BE the answer.

There is no position #2 when ChatGPT gives one recommendation.

**The shift in one example:**

**Old SEO:**  
"Digital marketing agency Cyprus | SEO services | PPC management"

**AI Search:**  
"We help B2B SaaS companies scale from $1M to $10M ARR using Answer Engine Optimization. Our clients show up in ChatGPT, not just Google."

See the difference?

One is optimized for crawlers. The other is optimized for intelligence.

**What to do:**

1/ Stop writing for algorithms. Write for humans (because AI now understands humans).

2/ Focus on authority signals, not ranking signals.

3/ Be specific about WHO you help and HOW. Generalists lose in AI search.

4/ Build credibility outside your website (third-party mentions matter more than ever).

If you're still stuffing keywords into meta tags, you're playing a game that ended two years ago.

The question isn't "what's my keyword strategy?"

It's "when someone asks AI for help, am I the answer they get?"
