## The Cyber Threat Landscape Facing Cyprus SMBs

Cyprus businesses operate in an increasingly digital environment — cloud tools, remote work, online payments — and that digital footprint creates more entry points for attackers. According to ENISA (the EU Agency for Cybersecurity), ransomware, phishing, and data breaches remain the top three threats across European SMBs, and Cyprus is no exception.

What makes local businesses particularly vulnerable:

- **Under-resourced IT teams.** Many Cyprus SMBs rely on a single in-house IT person or no dedicated IT support at all.
- **Outdated software and unpatched systems.** Legacy systems with known security gaps are a favourite target.
- **Lack of security awareness.** Employees clicking phishing emails remain the #1 entry point for attackers.
- **GDPR exposure.** A breach doesn't just cost you downtime — it can result in significant regulatory fines under EU data protection law.

The cost of a breach extends well beyond the ransom payment or data loss. Reputational damage, client trust erosion, and operational downtime can cripple a business for weeks.

## The Most Common Cyber Attacks Targeting Local Businesses

Understanding the threats is the first step to defending against them. Here are the attack types CDMA's team encounters most frequently with Cyprus clients:

**Phishing attacks** — Fraudulent emails impersonating banks, suppliers, or government agencies trick employees into handing over credentials or clicking malicious links. These are now highly personalised and convincing.

**Ransomware** — Malicious software encrypts your files and demands payment to restore access. Even if you pay, recovery is not guaranteed — and your data may already be sold.

**Business Email Compromise (BEC)** — Attackers hijack or spoof executive email accounts to authorise fraudulent wire transfers or data disclosures. Cyprus financial services firms are a prime target.

**Credential stuffing** — Stolen username/password combinations from other breaches are tested against your business systems. If your staff reuse passwords (and most do), this works.

**Insider threats** — Disgruntled employees or departing staff with retained access can cause significant damage, intentionally or not.

## Practical Steps to Protect Your Cyprus Business Right Now

You don't need an enterprise IT budget to meaningfully reduce your risk. Here's what every Cyprus business should have in place:

**1. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) everywhere.**  
Enable MFA on email, cloud platforms, and any system accessible remotely. This single step blocks the vast majority of credential-based attacks.

**2. Regular, tested backups.**  
Back up critical data daily to an off-site or cloud location — and test the restore process regularly. A backup you've never tested is a backup you can't trust.

**3. Patch and update consistently.**  
Software vulnerabilities are exploited within days of discovery. Establish a monthly patching cycle as a minimum, with critical patches applied immediately.

**4. Employee security awareness training.**  
Your people are your first line of defence. Regular, short training sessions on phishing recognition and password hygiene make a measurable difference.

**5. Endpoint detection and response (EDR).**  
Basic antivirus is no longer enough. Modern EDR tools monitor behaviour across devices and flag suspicious activity in real time.

**6. Access management.**  
Apply the principle of least privilege — employees should only have access to the data and systems they need for their role. Review and revoke access when staff leave.
