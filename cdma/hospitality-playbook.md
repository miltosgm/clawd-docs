## 1. Hospitality Sector Overview

### Why Hospitality Matters for CDMA

Cyprus is one of the Mediterranean's premier tourism destinations, welcoming over **3.5 million tourists annually** and generating roughly **€2.7 billion** in tourism revenue. The hospitality industry — hotels, resorts, boutique properties, and restaurants — is the backbone of the Cypriot economy.

This sector is increasingly technology-dependent. A hotel without reliable WiFi loses bookings. A restaurant with a slow POS during peak hours loses revenue and reputation. A resort hit by a cyberattack faces regulatory fines and permanent reputational damage.

CDMA is uniquely positioned to serve this sector because:
- CDMA is **Cyprus-based**, meaning local response, local relationships, and no language barriers
- CDMA offers **end-to-end managed IT**, not just hardware drops
- CDMA understands the **seasonal pressure** Cyprus hospitality businesses face (April–October peak season)

## 3. Key Pain Points & CDMA Solutions

### Pain Point 1: Unreliable WiFi — The #1 Guest Complaint

**The Problem:**
Hotels and restaurants rely on guest WiFi as a core amenity. Poor signal, slow speeds, or network crashes during peak hours directly impact TripAdvisor reviews and repeat bookings.

**Common Symptoms:**
- WiFi dead zones in certain rooms or outdoor areas
- Guest and staff networks not separated (security risk)
- Single ISP line with no failover
- Router/access point hardware from 5+ years ago

**CDMA's Solution:**
- Full **site WiFi survey** and coverage mapping before any deployment
- **Enterprise-grade access points** (Ubiquiti, Cisco Meraki, or equivalent) with centralised management
- **Network segmentation**: separate VLANs for guests, staff, POS, and management systems
- **Dual ISP failover** setup — if one line drops, traffic switches automatically
- **Guest WiFi portal** with branded login, usage limits, and analytics
- **Remote monitoring** with proactive alerts before issues become outages

**Talking ROI:** A single bad TripAdvisor review citing WiFi can cost 3–5 future bookings. CDMA's WiFi solution costs less than that lost revenue.

---

### Pain Point 2: POS Downtime During Peak Service

**The Problem:**
Restaurants and hotel F&B outlets lose thousands of euros per hour when their POS goes down on a busy Saturday night or during a peak tourist week. Most operators have no backup plan.

**Common Symptoms:**
- POS runs on old hardware with no maintenance contract
- Payment terminal disconnects from POS, forcing manual workarounds
- Kitchen display or printer integration fails
- No one to call at 9pm on a Friday

**CDMA's Solution:**
- **POS infrastructure audit** — hardware, connectivity, power, integration points
- Reliable **network backbone** ensuring POS always has connectivity
- **UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)** installation on critical POS hardware
- Partnerships with POS vendors for Tier 2 escalation support
- **Extended helpdesk hours** during peak season (April–October)
- **On-site SLA** with guaranteed response times

**Talking ROI:** A 2-hour POS outage during a busy dinner service can mean €2,000–€5,000 in lost revenue for a medium-sized restaurant. One CDMA contract pays for itself the first time it prevents that.

---

### Pain Point 3: Cybersecurity Exposure & GDPR Liability

**The Problem:**
Hotels and restaurants collect significant amounts of personal data — passport scans, credit card details, email addresses, loyalty profiles. They are prime targets for cyberattacks and face heavy GDPR fines if breached.

**Common Symptoms:**
- No firewall or outdated firewall hardware
- Staff using personal devices on the same network as POS/PMS
- No employee cybersecurity training
- Credit card data handled without PCI-DSS compliance
- GDPR data register not maintained

**CDMA's Solution:**
- **Network firewall** installation and configuration (Fortinet, SonicWall, or similar)
- **Endpoint detection and response (EDR)** on all staff devices
- **PCI-DSS readiness review** for any business handling card payments
- **GDPR compliance support** — data mapping, privacy policy, breach response plan
- **Staff cybersecurity awareness training** (annual or bi-annual)
- **Penetration testing** for larger properties
- **Dark web monitoring** for compromised credentials

**Talking ROI:** The average cost of a data breach for an SME in Europe is €200,000+. GDPR fines alone can reach 4% of annual turnover. CDMA's security stack is a fraction of that cost.

---

### Pain Point 4: No In-House IT = No One to Call

**The Problem:**
Most hospitality businesses in Cyprus don't have a dedicated IT person. When something breaks, the GM calls their nephew, a friend's IT contact, or just tries to restart the router. This reactive approach is costly and stressful.

**Common Symptoms:**
- Issues escalate because no one catches them early
- Response time from ad-hoc IT support is measured in days, not hours
- No documentation of systems, passwords, or configurations
- Software updates haven't been applied in months/years

**CDMA's Solution:**
- **Fully Managed IT** — CDMA acts as the client's IT department
- **Remote Monitoring & Management (RMM)** — issues detected and often resolved before the client notices
- **IT asset inventory and documentation** maintained by CDMA
- **Patch management** — systems kept up-to-date automatically
- **Dedicated account manager** — one point of contact for everything
- **Priority helpdesk access** via phone, email, or ticket portal

---

### Pain Point 5: Seasonal Peaks Create IT Stress

**The Problem:**
Cyprus hospitality runs at 30% capacity in winter and 95%+ in summer. IT infrastructure needs to handle that swing. Most businesses don't plan for it.

**Common Symptoms:**
- WiFi collapses when the hotel fills up in July
- POS queues slow down with high transaction volumes
- Extra seasonal staff get added without IT provisioning plan
- Temporary pop-up bars or beach clubs set up with no proper connectivity

**CDMA's Solution:**
- **Capacity planning** as part of annual IT review
- **Scalable cloud infrastructure** for seasonal demand spikes
- **Rapid provisioning** for seasonal staff accounts and devices
- **Temporary site connectivity** for pop-up venues, events, weddings
- Pre-season **IT health check** to identify risks before peak season starts

## 6. Onboarding Process for New Hospitality Clients

### Overview
CDMA's onboarding process is designed to be fast, low-disruption, and confidence-building. The goal: client is fully under management within **10–15 business days** and feels the value within the first 30 days.

---

### Phase 1: Discovery & Audit (Days 1–3)

**Goal:** Understand the client's existing IT environment and identify quick wins.

**Activities:**
- [ ] Kick-off call with GM / IT Manager / relevant decision-maker
- [ ] Site visit — physical walkthrough of IT infrastructure (server room, network equipment, workstations, POS, WiFi)
- [ ] IT asset inventory (hardware, software, licences)
- [ ] Network diagram creation (or update if existing)
- [ ] Security posture assessment (firewall, antivirus, patch levels)
- [ ] WiFi coverage spot-check
- [ ] Review of existing vendor contracts (ISP, PMS, POS)
- [ ] Documentation of critical credentials (stored securely in CDMA's password vault)

**Deliverable:** IT Audit Report with findings, risk rating, and recommended actions

---

### Phase 2: Baseline & Onboarding Setup (Days 4–7)

**Goal:** Get all monitoring and management tools live.

**Activities:**
- [ ] Deploy RMM agents on all managed devices
- [ ] Configure network monitoring (routers, switches, APs)
- [ ] Set up centralised alerting and escalation rules
- [ ] Install/verify endpoint protection on all managed devices
- [ ] Configure backup solutions (cloud + local where applicable)
- [ ] Create client account in helpdesk portal
- [ ] Onboard all relevant staff contacts into ticketing system
- [ ] Brief on-site staff on how to log IT issues

**Deliverable:** All systems live in CDMA management dashboard; client receives portal login

---

### Phase 3: Quick Wins & Remediation (Days 8–12)

**Goal:** Resolve high-priority risks identified in the audit.

**Activities:**
- [ ] Address critical vulnerabilities (patch, configure, replace as needed)
- [ ] Implement network segmentation if not present (guest/staff/POS VLANs)
- [ ] Upgrade/replace any end-of-life hardware flagged in audit
- [ ] Configure WiFi guest portal if included in package
- [ ] Establish ISP failover if included in package
- [ ] POS connectivity check and stability confirmation
- [ ] UPS installation on critical systems where needed

**Deliverable:** Remediation complete; updated risk register shared with client

---

### Phase 4: Handover & Go-Live (Days 13–15)

**Goal:** Full operational handover; client is confident and supported.

**Activities:**
- [ ] Final walkthrough with client's key contacts
- [ ] Handover of network diagram and IT asset register
- [ ] Confirm escalation matrix (who calls whom, and when)
- [ ] SLA document signed and shared
- [ ] Introduction to dedicated account manager
- [ ] Staff briefing on IT support procedures (how to raise tickets)
- [ ] 30-day check-in call scheduled

**Deliverable:** Signed SLA, full documentation package, client goes live on CDMA management

---

### Phase 5: 30-Day Review (Day 30)

**Goal:** Confirm satisfaction, surface any gaps, build the relationship.

**Activities:**
- [ ] Review first 30 days — tickets raised, issues resolved, monitoring alerts
- [ ] Client satisfaction check-in
- [ ] Review any outstanding remediation items
- [ ] Plan for pre-season health check if applicable
- [ ] Upsell/expand conversation if natural (e.g. adding security package, WiFi upgrade)

**Deliverable:** 30-day report; updated roadmap for next 6 months
